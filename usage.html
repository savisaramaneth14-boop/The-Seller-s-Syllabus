The Usage of Information Technology in Business

In 2026 Information Technology or IT is not a support function anymore. It is, like the system of a business. IT connects every department through a system usually called an Enterprise Resource Planning system or ERP. This helps to make sure data moves quickly across the organization. The ERP system helps connect all the departments. Information Technology makes it all work.

Accounting and IT

Information Technology has automated the parts of accounting allowing accounting professionals to focus on strategy.

  • Artificial Intelligence Automation: Systems now use Machine Learning to categorize transactions extract data from invoices and flag fraudulent activity without any help from people.
  • Real Time Reporting: of waiting for a monthly close Information Technology provides real time dashboards that show exactly how much cash the business has at any given moment.
  • Strategic Advisory: Accounting professionals use predictive Information Technology tools to forecast cash flow helping the business decide when to invest in a new thing. Accounting professionals use Information Technology to help the business make decisions, about money. Information Technology is really important for accounting professionals to do their jobs well.

Human Resource and IT

Information technology has changed resources from a place that just kept files to a place that helps make plans for the people who work at the company.

  • Some companies use a kind of intelligence called Agentic AI. This artificial intelligence helps human resources do tasks like scheduling interviews all over the world signing people up for benefits and answering questions from employees at any time of day.
  • These companies also use something called Skills Ontology. This is a part of the computer system that looks at resumes and the history of projects that people have worked on. It makes a kind of map of what each employee's good at. This helps managers find the person for a new project inside the company very quickly.
  • Some companies can even predict when an employee is getting too tired or stressed out. They use computer programs that look at things like how many hours people are working. This is, like looking at someones body language to see how they are feeling. If the computer program sees that someone is working much it can send a warning to human resources before the person gets too burned out or decides to quit their job.

Production and IT

In a factory or on a service floor, IT helps connect the physical worlds.

  • Digital Twins: Engineers make a 3D copy of a machine or process. They test changes in the world first so they do not make costly mistakes on real equipment.
  • IoT Sensors: Sensors, on machines track temperature and vibration. They send data to the backend to predict when a machine might break down.
  • Autonomous Workflows: If a machine detects a worn-out part the IT system orders a replacement. Schedules a technician. This happens automatically without anyone needing to make a phone call.

Marketing,Sales and IT

Marketing is not about guessing what people want anymore. It is about knowing what they actually need.

  • Customer Data Platforms are really helpful. They bring together all the information from media, emails and the website. This gives us a view of the customer, which is often called a "360-degree" view of the customer.
  • Micro-Audiences are another thing. Artificial intelligence uses the customer data to send offers to customers. For example if a customer looks at a product three times they might get a discount code.
  • Omnichannel Sales is also very useful. It makes sure that all the different ways we sell things are connected. So if a customer buys something on our app, which we call "Click" the people, at our store, which we call "Brick" will know about it. They will not try to sell the customer the thing again the next day. This helps Customer Data Platforms and Micro-Audiences to work better for the customer.

Supply Chain Management and IT

IT helps keep an eye on goods moving around the world.

  • Real-Time Tracking: We use IoT and GPS to track shipments and check their condition. For example we can see if a refrigerated truck is getting too warm.
  • Blockchain: It creates a record of a products journey. This is important, for proving where "organic" food comes from or verifying high-value electronics are genuine.
  • Smart Contracts: When a shipment arrives our system automatically pays the supplier. This removes weeks of delay caused by paperwork.

Business Communication and IT

Communication has gone beyond just sending emails. Now we have places online where people can work together.

  • There are something called Unified Platforms. These are like offices where you can chat with your team share files and work on projects all in one place. You can use things like Slack or Microsoft Teams for this.
  • Then there is Augmented Reality. This is really cool. Teams in countries can meet up in a virtual room like Horizon Workrooms and use special characters to talk to each other. Some people even use glasses that let them see what someone else is seeing so they can help them fix something.
  • There is also something called Centralized Truth. This means that when one team updates some information, like a clients phone number it automatically updates for all the teams. This helps prevent mistakes and makes sure everyone is, on the page. For example when the Sales team updates a clients phone number the Support and Billing teams will see the number right away so they do not have to deal with old information. This way we can avoid what we call "miscommunication silos". Make sure Communication and the Communication process is smooth.
